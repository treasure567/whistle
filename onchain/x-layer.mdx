X Layer is OKK's Ethereum Layer 2, built on Polygon CDK. It settles to Ethereum mainnet while keeping gas costs a fraction of L1 — which matters when every in-tournament prediction, roster submission, and commemorative mint should feel instant, not expensive.

## Why X Layer for whistle

A World Cup produces dozens of matches over a month. Each match can trigger position opens, settlement calls, NFT mints, and fantasy roster finalizations — all onchain. A slow or expensive chain would make that unworkable for everyday fans.

X Layer fits because:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Low fees" icon="coins">
    Submitting a prediction or minting a Moment costs a few cents in gas, not dollars.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fast finality" icon="shield">
    Transactions confirm in seconds, so agents can act before odds shift mid-match.
  </Card>
  <Card title="OKB-native" icon="wallet">
    Native OKB is the gas token, and test OKB funds your agents — no cross-chain wrapping needed.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

When you connect your wallet in whistle, the app requests a network switch or add automatically via wagmi. You will see a single confirmation prompt in your wallet — no manual RPC entry required.

## Two flavours of OKB

This is the most common point of confusion for new users.

<Note>
There are two tokens called "OKB" in the whistle environment. They serve different purposes and are not interchangeable.
</Note>

**Native OKB (gas token)**
: This is the L2 gas token shown in your wallet balance. You need a small amount to pay for every transaction on X Layer — funding an agent, approving a token, minting an NFT. Obtain it from the X Layer testnet faucet.

**Test OKB — MockERC20 (funding token)**
: This is an open-mint ERC-20 deployed at `0x487F536593b1680B8247E67254Fc8D0394D137D7` specifically for the hackathon testnet. It acts as the stablecoin you use to fund agents and back predictions. Anyone can mint it freely. It carries 18 decimals and is **not** deployed to mainnet.

```
MockERC20 (test OKB)  0x487F536593b1680B8247E67254Fc8D0394D137D7
```

When the UI shows "Fund Emma" or "Allocate to Jack", the amount is denominated in this MockERC20 OKB, not in native gas OKB.

## Connecting your wallet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open whistle and click Connect">
    The app uses wagmi. If X Layer Testnet (chainId 1952) is not already in your wallet, a network-add prompt appears automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve the network">
    Confirm the RPC details in your wallet popup. You will not need to enter them manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch to X Layer Testnet">
    A second prompt asks you to switch the active network. After confirming, your wallet is live on X Layer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your balances">
    You need native OKB for gas and MockERC20 OKB for funding. Mint test OKB from the contract or the in-app faucet button before proceeding.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Keep at least 0.01 native OKB in your wallet at all times. The funding flow involves three transactions (mint, approve, allocate) and each one consumes a small amount of gas.
</Tip>

## Verifying transactions

Every transaction whistle submits is visible on the public block explorer. Paste any transaction hash or contract address into the explorer to inspect it.
