The prediction screen lives at `/play/predict`. You can build a slip yourself
from structured market categories, or hand the decision to Jack: give him a
budget, a risk level, and any preferences, and he returns a budgeted slip
ready to approve and fund onchain.

See [Jack the Bookie](/agents/jack) for how he reads a tie and prices markets.

## Manual market builder

The manual builder lets you construct a slip from six market categories:

| Category | Example markets |
|---|---|
| Match | Result (1X2), Both teams to score, Clean sheet |
| Goals | Over/under 2.5, First goalscorer, Anytime scorer |
| Corners | Total corners over/under, First corner, Asian corners |
| Cards | Total cards over/under, First booking, Player to be carded |
| Fouls | Total fouls over/under |
| Specials | MOTM, Penalty in match, VAR decision |

Select one or more legs, review the combined slip, then move to the funding
step when you're ready.

## Ask Jack — the budgeted slip

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Ask Jack">
    Switch to the **Ask Jack** tab on the predict screen. You will see three
    inputs: budget (in OKB), risk level (low / medium / high), and an
    optional preferences field (e.g. "favour corners markets", "no
    accumulators").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Jack builds your slip">
    Submitting calls `POST /v1/predictions/slip` with your parameters. Jack
    reads the upcoming fixtures, prices each leg against his model, and
    returns a slip that fits inside your stated budget and risk band.

    ```json
    {
      "budget": "50",
      "risk": "medium",
      "preferences": "favour goals markets"
    }
    ```

    The response includes each leg with a label, odds, and stake, plus a
    total stake that does not exceed your budget.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the slip">
    Jack's slip is displayed as a readable card — each leg labelled, odds
    shown, total stake confirmed. You can remove individual legs before
    approving.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve and fund">
    Hit **Fund slip**. The app calls the onchain funding flow:
    approve the `PositionManager` to spend your OKB, then call
    `PositionManager.allocate(2, amount)` (Jack's onchain agent ID is **2**)
    to commit the stake. Both transactions are signed in your browser via
    wagmi — whistle never holds your keys.

    See [Fund](/play/fund) for the full transaction walkthrough.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Jack's slip carries `source: "llm"` when the model generated it and
  `source: "heuristic"` when the deterministic fallback ran. Either way you
  get a valid, budgeted slip — the source badge is displayed so you know
  which engine produced it.
</Note>

## Reading a match before you bet

Before placing any prediction, Jack can give you a full read of the tie.
From the match list, click a fixture and select **Jack's read** to call
`POST /v1/matches/read`. This returns his assessment of the match: form,
head-to-head, key players, and which markets look most interesting. You can
then open a live chat with Jack (`POST /v1/matches/chat`) to ask follow-up
questions before committing a slip.

<Tip>
  Use the read before you open the manual builder — Jack often flags markets
  (corners, cards) that the headline result odds miss. His read is free and
  does not require a funded position.
</Tip>

## The slip onchain

When you fund a slip the OKB moves onchain to the `PositionManager` under
Jack's agent ID. The transaction is public on the
[X Layer testnet explorer](https://www.okx.com/web3/explorer/xlayer-test).
Your wallet balance (shown in the navbar) updates after the `allocate`
transaction confirms.

<Warning>
  whistle uses a test OKB token (MockERC20 at
  `0x487F536593b1680B8247E67254Fc8D0394D137D7`) — open-mint, no real value.
  The native OKB shown as your wallet balance is the X Layer gas token and
  is separate from the MockERC20 used to fund agents and bets.
</Warning>
